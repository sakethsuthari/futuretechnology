1. Voice Assistants - What Is It?

Voice Assistant has made our lives a lot easier. A Voice Assistant can also be called as an intelligent assistant. An intelligent personal assistant can help someone with basic tasks. They often understand natural language and can help

with things like calling people, creating meeting requests, reporting a sports score, and sharing the weather forecast. Intelligent personal assistants have access to a large amount of information on a device or online, which enables them to perform simple tasks.The long-term vision for voice assistants is to act as a smart bridge between humans and the vast knowledge and capacities which the internet delivers. Taking away the need to use any device or screen to interact with the internet, technology or other humans in different locations.

It saves a lot of time, as we are able to browse everything with our voices only, while doing some other work at the same time, making us multitaskers. The most popular Voice assistants are Google Echo, Amazon Alexa, Microsoft Cortana, Apple Siri

1. Hologram Chat - What Is It?

Holographic telepresence is an evolving technology for full-motion, three-dimensional (3D) video conferencing.Holographic telepresence systems can project realistic, full-motion, real-time 3D images of distant people and objects into a room,

along with real-time audio communication, with a level of realism rivaling physical presence.In telemedicine, for example, telepresence can allow medical professionals to advise and assist colleagues thousands of miles away in real time. The technology can also reduce the necessity of travel for business meetings and facilitate distance education. Other potential applications include enhanced movies and television programming, advertising, gaming, 3D mapping, aerospace navigation, robot control, and various other forms of simulation.

1. Humanoid Robots - What Is It?

Robots are very active and can work tirelessly for decades without a single error. Humanoid robots are professional service robots built to mimic human motion and interaction. Like all service robots, they provide value by

automating tasks in a way that leads to cost-savings and productivity. Humanoid robots are a relatively new form of professional service robot.Robots are widely used in such industries as automobile manufacture to perform simple repetitive tasks, and in industries where work must be performed in environments hazardous to humans. .Sophia is a social humanoid robot developed by Hong Kong-based company Hanson Robotics.Humanoid robots are staffing a hospital in Wuhan, China, helping medics to disinfect, measure temperatures, deliver food and medicine etc.

Robots are delivering everything from online orders to room service.Medical facilities including the famed Mayo Clinic use robotics to operate on patients. Using robotics provides “more precision, flexibility, and control than is possible with conventional techniques.”

1. Auto-Pilot - What Is It?

An autopilot is a device used to guide an aircraft without direct assistance from the pilot. Early autopilots were only able to maintain a constant heading and altitude, but modern autopilots are capable of controlling every part of the

flight envelope from just after take-off to landing.The autopilot system relies on a series of sensors around the aircraft that pick up information like speed, altitude and turbulence. That data are ingested into the computer, which then makes the necessary changes. Basically, it can do almost everything a pilot can do. Key phrase: almost everything. Before takeoff, the pilot will enter the route into the computer, giving it a start and end position and exactly how to get there. Throughout that route there are a series of points that the computer will note, each

having its own speed and altitude. The autopilot does not steer the airplane on the ground or taxi the plane at the gate. Generally, the pilot will handle takeoff and then initiate the autopilot to take over for most of the flight. In some newer aircraft models, autopilot systems will even land the plane.
